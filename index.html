About

Currently, I am working as a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Biological Sciences at Mississippi State University. My research investigates how environmental gradients, nutrient inputs, and management strategies influence microbial communities and ecosystem function across both coastal and agricultural landscapes..

My current project examines the transport and fate of bacterial communities in Mississippi coastal ecosystems, linking community composition and function to hydrological and physicochemical drivers across estuarine gradients.

During my Ph.D in Agronomy and Computational Biology at Mississippi State University, I investigated how different agricultural management practices, biotic and abiotic stresses influence on soil microbial diversity, network stability, and carbon-nitrogen cycling in Mississippi production systems like corn, soybean, other legume crops and tea. My broader interests lie in microbial ecology, bioinformatics, and multi-omics integration to advance predictive understanding of ecosystem processes under environmental change.
